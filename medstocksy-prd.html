01 — Overview

Product Overview

Medstocksy is a comprehensive, open-source web-based inventory management system purpose-built for pharmacies, medical stores, and chemist shops. Built with a React + TypeScript frontend and Supabase as its backend-as-a-service, it replaces paper-based pharmacy workflows with a real-time digital system covering inventory tracking, GST billing, customer CRM, and WhatsApp automation.

The codebase at github.com/Arham0003/Medstocksy has 34 commits, is 91.5% TypeScript, includes PL/pgSQL stored procedures (5.2%), and is deployed on Vercel at medstocksy.vercel.app (→ app.medstocksy.in).

✦
Core Value: Replace manual pharmacy workflows — paper ledgers, notebook customer records, untracked expiry — with a real-time SaaS system that automates every step from stock entry to patient refill reminders.
🎯
Business Goals
  • Digitise pharmacy operations currently on paper or legacy desktop software
  • Reduce inventory waste from untracked expiry dates
  • Increase repeat revenue through automated CRM and WhatsApp reminders
  • Provide actionable analytics — margins, turnover, top products
⚙️
Technical Goals
  • SPA with client-side routing via React Router; no page reloads
  • Real-time inventory updates via Supabase Realtime subscriptions
  • Google OAuth + email/password auth with DB-level RLS isolation
  • Zero-infrastructure deployment — Vercel (frontend) + Supabase (backend)

03 — Problem

Problem Statement

India's ~10 lakh registered medical stores manage ₹3 lakh crore in annual retail, yet the vast majority use paper ledgers, Excel files, or outdated local software. This creates four compounding failures that Medstocksy directly addresses:

📦
Inventory Blindness

No real-time stock visibility. Pharmacies overstock (wasting capital) or run out mid-sale. Manual stock counts are infrequent and error-prone.

💸
Lost Refill Revenue

Chronic patients are 60–70% of pharmacy revenue. Without prescription tracking or automated reminders, they silently shift to competitors.

⏳
Expired Stock Waste

Medicines expire on shelves undetected. Financial loss and patient safety risk are severe, especially for small pharmacies with limited capital.

📊
Zero Business Insights

No understanding of margins, top-sellers, seasonal demand, or high-value customers. All decisions are intuition-based with no data backing.

04 — Features

Feature Modules

Five modules derived directly from the repository structure, source code, and documentation files.

🏪
Module 1 — Inventory Management

Core module. Every product tracked in real-time. Stock auto-decrements on sale, increments on purchase or return.

  • Add / edit / delete products: name, batch, MRP, purchase price, manufacturer, HSN code
  • Real-time stock level with unit of measure; updates on every transaction
  • Low-stock threshold per product — dashboard alert when stock ≤ threshold
  • Expiry date tracking per batch — alerts at 30 / 60 / 90 day lead times
  • Medicine search by name, composition, manufacturer
  • Stock adjustment with reason log (damage, theft, reconciliation)
  • 5,400+ medicine catalog entries
🧾
Module 2 — Sales & Billing

Point-of-sale module. GST-compliant invoicing auto-updates inventory and links to patient CRM. Sales return flow tracked and fixed via PL/pgSQL migration (see repo docs).

  • Create sale with multiple line items; inline medicine search
  • GST invoice: CGST/SGST/IGST breakdown, HSN codes, GSTIN field
  • Link every sale to a customer profile at checkout
  • Automatic inventory deduction on confirmed sale
  • Sales return flow with stock re-addition (bug fixed — FIX_SALES_RETURN_STOCK.md)
  • Multiple payment modes: cash, UPI, card, credit
  • Invoice PDF — print or share via WhatsApp
  • Daily cash register summary and shift reconciliation
💬
Module 3 — Customer CRM

Turns transactional visits into ongoing patient relationships via WhatsApp automation and prescription tracking.

  • Customer profiles: name, phone, address, chronic flag
  • Full purchase history per customer — all invoices, products, dates
  • Prescription logging: medicine, dosage, frequency, refill interval
  • Automated WhatsApp refill reminder at prescription refill schedule
  • WhatsApp delivery status tracking: sent / delivered / read
  • Customer segmentation: chronic, walk-in, high-value
  • Patient retention rate analytics
📊
Module 4 — Analytics & Reports

Data-driven insights for procurement, pricing, and operations decisions.

  • Real-time dashboard: daily sales, active transactions, low stock count, WhatsApp alerts sent
  • Sales trend reports: daily / weekly / monthly / custom range
  • Top products by revenue, quantity, and gross margin
  • Profit & Loss with per-product margin
  • Inventory reports: turnover, slow-moving stock, dead stock
  • Expiry risk report: rupee value at risk from near-expiry batches
  • Export to PDF and CSV
🔐
Module 5 — Auth & User Management

Supabase Auth with Google OAuth and email/password. Row-Level Security enforces tenant isolation at database level.

  • Google Sign-In (OAuth 2.0) — Vercel redirect issue fixed in recent commits
  • Email + password with email verification and OTP reset
  • Role-based access: owner (full), manager (no settings), staff (billing only)
  • Multi-device concurrent sessions supported
  • Supabase RLS: every table isolated by user_id = auth.uid()
  • .env secrets: VITE_SUPABASE_URL, VITE_SUPABASE_ANON_KEY, Razorpay key

10 — Authentication

Authentication Flow

🔑
Google OAuth 2.0
  • User clicks "Sign in with Google"
  • Supabase Auth initiates OAuth 2.0 with Google provider
  • Google consent screen → user approves
  • Callback to Supabase → JWT issued → redirect to app
  • Vercel redirect URI issue fixed in recent commits (documented in repo)
  • Improved error handling and auth error reporting added
📧
Email / Password
  • Sign-up with email + password → Supabase sends verification email
  • User clicks verify link → account activated
  • Sign-in → Supabase validates → JWT + refresh token issued
  • Auto-refresh handled by Supabase JS client library
  • Password reset via email OTP link
  • Sessions persist in localStorage
🛡️
Row Level Security (RLS)

Every table has RLS policies. The user_id column is the isolation key. A pharmacy can only access rows where user_id = auth.uid(). This prevents cross-tenant data access at DB level — no application-layer enforcement needed.

  • SELECT policy: user_id = auth.uid() on every table
  • INSERT policy: auto-sets user_id = auth.uid()
  • UPDATE / DELETE policy: only row owner
  • Only the anon key is exposed to the frontend via VITE env vars — service-role key stays server-side

Public API planned for pharmacy chain integrations with existing billing software or POS systems. Will expose inventory, sales, and customer endpoints with API key auth. Target Phase 3.

14 — Risks

Risks & Mitigations

High
Supabase vendor lock-in
Entire backend (DB, auth, realtime) on Supabase. Pricing or service changes are costly to migrate away from. Mitigation: Abstract DB calls in service layer; keep SQL migrations portable in /supabase folder.
High
Google OAuth redirect failures (known recurrence)
Already occurred on Vercel deployment — fixed and documented in recent commits. Mitigation: Document all redirect URIs in VERCEL_DEPLOYMENT.md; test Google auth on every production deploy.
High
Sales return stock bug recurrence
Bug caused stock not to re-add on returns (see DEBUG_RETURN_ISSUE.sql). Fixed via PL/pgSQL migration. Mitigation: comprehensive-test.cjs regression suite; DB trigger to validate stock integrity on return.
High
WhatsApp Business API policy changes
Meta can change WhatsApp API pricing or template approval rules with short notice. Mitigation: Build SMS fallback channel; email reminders as secondary; abstract messaging layer from CRM logic.
Med
Low-tech user adoption
Many pharmacists have limited digital experience. Mitigation: Video onboarding, WhatsApp-based customer support, Hindi UI (Phase 2), in-app tooltips, ≤10 min onboarding goal.
Med
DPDPA 2023 — patient data liability
Prescription and patient health data require explicit consent, minimization, and erasure rights. Mitigation: Consent flow at signup, data minimization policy, legal review of data handling practices.
Low
Razorpay payment failure on renewal
Payment failures suspend subscriptions. Mitigation: 3-day grace period; retry logic via Razorpay webhooks; SMS/email dunning flow before suspension.
15 — Compliance

Compliance & Legal

⚖️
DPDPA 2023

Digital Personal Data Protection Act. Patient health data requires explicit consent, purpose limitation, data minimization, and right-to-erasure. Supabase RLS enforces per-user isolation. Consent logged at account creation.

🔐
IT Act 2000

Electronic records compliance. Digital invoices constitute valid records under Section 4. Data security obligations under Section 43A. AES-256 encryption at rest + TLS 1.3 in transit.

🧾
GST Compliance

Invoices include GSTIN, HSN codes, correct slab rates (0%, 5%, 12% on medicines), CGST/SGST/IGST breakdown. B2B and B2C invoice rules applied. E-invoice mandate threshold compliance required above ₹5 crore turnover.

💊
Pharmacy Regulations

Platform does not verify pharmacy licenses or prescription validity. Users must comply with the Drugs & Cosmetics Act, Pharmacy Act 1948, and state pharmacy board rules independently. Medstocksy is a software tool only.

⚠
Legal Disclaimer (per Terms of Service): Medstocksy does not dispense medical advice, verify prescriptions, or ensure regulatory compliance on the pharmacy's behalf. All legal, licensing, and operational obligations rest with the pharmacy owner/operator. Governing law: Indian courts in Lucknow, UP.
